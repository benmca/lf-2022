I've been listening back to all of the Zappa Synclavier material, intending to transcribe and cover (or borrow) from it sometime soon. [Little Beige Sambo]() has been a favorite of mine, from his excellent [Frank Zappa Meets the Mothers of Prevention]() album. It goes by so fast you can barely track, but the rhtyhms have always worked for me. 

Out on a run today I thought "Frank gave us a gift: there's a constant pulse in this piece", which makes it very easy to throw on a grid. So that's what I did. Using Ableton Live to bring it down to 60 bpm, now I have a hope of transcribing the lines.

The samples are really blah, but I can hear him thinking of his band: there's clearly a hammond, bass, percussionist and drummer, of course. There are many grooves happening at this tempo I couldn't hear at full speed - more of this kind of thing to come. 

Pictured: a huge moon rose tonight as I drove home from the run I mentioned.
